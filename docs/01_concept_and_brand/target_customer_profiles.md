# Target Customer Profiles

## Purpose
These profiles guide programming, pricing, and marketing for both the Phoenix flagship and franchise locations. They highlight motivations, friction points, and how Co.work.play Café solves for them.

## Remote Work Parents (Core)
- **Who they are:** Dual-income households with children under 6; hybrid/remote roles in tech, healthcare admin, design, or education.
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** Secure 2–4 hours of focused work while kids are nearby; avoid unreliable childcare or noisy coffee shops.
- **Pain points:** Childcare gaps, unpredictable noise, lack of ergonomic seating, limited healthy food options.
- **What wins them:** Guaranteed play supervision, reliable Wi-Fi and power, phone booths, membership discounts on café orders, easy booking of meeting rooms.
- **Marketing cues:** “Get work done while they play,” employer stipend-friendly memberships, weekday promos tied to school calendars.

## Freelancers & Micro Teams
- **Who they are:** Designers, therapists, consultants, and micro agencies (2–6 people) who need occasional meeting space and prefer community over traditional coworking.
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** Host clients, collaborate briefly, and tap into weekday energy without a long-term lease.
- **Pain points:** Paying for unused office space; difficulty coordinating with teammates who are also parents.
- **What wins them:** Hourly/day meeting room packages, café catering, priority booking, member storage lockers, and childcare add-ons during sprint days.
- **Marketing cues:** “Client-ready rooms with coffee handled,” team packs with bundled play credits.

## Caregivers & Grandparents
- **Who they are:** Primary caregivers, grandparents, and nannies responsible for daytime activities.
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** Safe indoor play, socialization for kids, and a comfortable place to read or manage errands.
- **Pain points:** Loud chaotic play centers, lack of seating, poor cleanliness, and limited healthy food for kids.
- **What wins them:** Friendly attendants who learn children’s names, low-sugar menu options, stroller parking, clean restrooms, and predictable schedules.
- **Marketing cues:** “Clean, calm play with coffee for you,” loyalty stamps for weekday visits.

## Neighborhood Families & Event Hosts
- **Who they are:** Local families planning birthdays, reunions, or recurring classes (music, language, art).
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** Turn-key party hosting with themed décor, simple catering, and stress-free clean-up.
- **Pain points:** Complex booking, add-on surprises, and inconsistent staffing at party venues.
- **What wins them:** Flat-fee party packages, clear capacities, pre-set themes, digital invites, and a dedicated party captain.
- **Marketing cues:** “Parties that start and end on time,” “members save on every celebration.”

## Employers & Community Partners
- **Who they are:** Companies offering caregiver benefits, neighborhood associations, and pediatric wellness providers.
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** Provide family-friendly perks, host small activations, or reach local parents.
- **Pain points:** Low engagement of generic perks; lack of authentic community venues.
- **What wins them:** Corporate membership bundles, co-branded events, cross-promotions with wellness/education partners, and reporting on utilization.
- **Marketing cues:** “Support caregivers without building a daycare,” “sponsor a parent cowork morning.”

## Messaging Guardrails
- Lead with productivity and safety; follow with hospitality and community.
- Avoid daycare positioning; emphasize supervised play, not childcare replacement.
- Keep visuals calm, modern, and inclusive across family structures and abilities.
