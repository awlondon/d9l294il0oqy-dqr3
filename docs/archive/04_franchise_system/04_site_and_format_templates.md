# Site and Format Templates

## Neighborhood Co.work.PLAY (~5–6k sf)
- Emphasis on efficient play hall and compact cafe; strong parking.
- Layout priorities: 60–70% of floor area for play/cafe, 30–40% for cowork and support. Outdoor patio where zoning allows.
- Capacity targets: 120–150 play guests at peak weekend dayparts; 40–50 cowork seats (mix of open tables and phone booths).
- Typical site adjacencies: grocery-anchored centers, family fitness, pediatric services, and charter schools.

## Urban Compact (~4–5k sf)
- Dense seating, multi-use rooms that flex between party and cowork functions; premium visibility and transit access.
- Layout priorities: vertical circulation clarity (elevator/stroller storage), sound separation for cowork, and strong stroller/bike parking.
- Capacity targets: 80–110 play guests; 50–60 cowork seats with higher daypass mix.
- Typical site adjacencies: transit corridors, Class A/B office, multifamily clusters with young families.

## Flagship / Destination (~8–9k sf)
- Full program with larger party/event space, expanded cafe, and more generous circulation.
- Layout priorities: dedicated birthday suites, party pantry, and event AV; separate toddler wing to reduce congestion.
- Capacity targets: 170–220 play guests; 70–90 cowork seats including reservable micro offices.
- Typical site adjacencies: regional lifestyle centers, premium suburban nodes, and campuses with strong childcare demand.

## Site evaluation checklist (applies to all formats)

- Visibility and access: prominent signage, safe drop-off, and stroller-friendly entries.
- Utilities: 400–600 amps power, 2" water line, 4" grease waste for cafe, and rooftop/sidewall venting path.
- Acoustics and structure: slab capable of play structure anchoring, ceiling heights 12–16 ft clear, and minimal columns in play area.
- Fire/life safety: sprinklers, egress paths that allow controlled access to play without blocking exits, and clear ADA routes.
