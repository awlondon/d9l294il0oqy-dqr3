# Phoenix Flagship – Five-Year Financial Summary (Outline)

> This document is a narrative companion to the full Excel/Sheets model.  
> Actual numbers should be updated to match the latest model.

## 1. High-Level Assumptions

- **Store size**: ~8,500 SF.  
- **Opening**: early 2026.  
- **CapEx**: approximately $2.2M (build-out, equipment, soft costs, contingency).  
- **Funding scenario**: core scenario assumes a lead investor funds CapEx, with founders retaining operating and franchisor equity.

Operational assumptions:

- Membership ramp over first 18–24 months.  
- Gradual increase in day passes and party/event bookings.  
- Café sales correlated with member and guest traffic.

---

## 2. Revenue Mix (Indicative Structure)

By Year 3–5, an illustrative mix (percent of total revenue):

- Memberships & passes: **~40–50%**  
- Café & retail sales: **~25–35%**  
- Parties & events: **~10–20%**  
- Miscellaneous (classes, corporate partnerships): **~5–10%**

## 4. Ramp & Stabilization Narrative

- **Year 1**  
  - Pre-opening marketing and founding member offers.  
  - Lower utilization in early months, rising toward break-even.  

- **Year 2**  
  - Membership and party/event volume maturing.  
  - Café and retail attachment rates improving via better menu and operations.

- **Year 3–5**  
  - Flagship operates as a stable, high-performing unit.  
  - Focus shifts to content creation, training franchisees, and optimizing margins.
