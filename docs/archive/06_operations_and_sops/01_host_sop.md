# Host / Front-of-House SOP  
Co.work.PLAY Café

## 1. Role Purpose

The Host is the **first and last impression** of Co.work.PLAY Café.

They are responsible for:

- Greeting every adult and child with warmth and clarity.
- Managing check-in for **members, reservations, and guest day passes**.
- Helping control flow into the play areas and cowork zones.
- Coordinating with the Play Attendant and Café team to keep the experience smooth and safe.

The Host is not “just a cashier” – they are the **traffic controller** and **vibe keeper**.

---

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Welcome and orient all arrivals.
- Verify memberships and reservations.
- Sell and process guest day passes.
- Explain basic house rules and play policies.
- Issue wristbands / labels and log child check-ins.
- Direct guests to:
  - Play areas,
  - Cowork zones, and
  - Café/bar.
- Manage waitlist when at capacity.
- Notify Manager of incidents, complaints, or safety concerns.
- Maintain a clean, tidy front entry.

---

## 3. Standards of Service

**Tone & presence**

- Warm, calm, and clear.
- Speak at a pace that is easy for tired, stressed parents to follow.
- Make eye contact with both adults and children (kid-level friendliness).

**Appearance**

- Uniform / dress code as defined by HQ.
- Name tag visible.
- No personal calls or phones at the host stand.

---

## 4. Check-in Flows

### 4.1 Member – No Reservation

1. **Greeting**  
   - “Hi, welcome to Co.work.PLAY Café! Are you a member with us today?”

2. **Lookup**  
   - Search by last name, phone, or membership ID.
   - Confirm membership status and allowed children / caregivers.

3. **Children & play**  
   - Confirm which children are present.
   - Check for notes: allergies, special instructions.

4. **Access setup**  
   - Apply wristbands / labels to adult and child with matching code.
   - Mark play area access in the system (time in).

5. **Orientation** (if new / infrequent)  
   - Point out:
     - Play areas and age zones.
     - Cowork areas (quiet vs open).
     - Café ordering location.
     - Restrooms / nursing-friendly spaces.

6. **Handoff**  
   - If child is going straight into play room:
     - Radio or verbally notify Play Attendant:
       - “New member: [Child name + age], [brief note].”
   - Politely direct adult to Café or Cowork.

---

### 4.2 Member – With Reservation (Play or Party)

1. **Greeting & confirm reservation**  
   - “Welcome back! Are you checking in for your [play block / party] today?”

2. **Locate reservation**  
   - Verify time, child name(s), party host if applicable.

3. **Check-in**  
   - Same steps as member flow (bands, notes, orientation).
   - For parties, confirm room assignment and timing:
     - Start/end time,
     - Food/cake timing,
     - Any special instructions.

4. **Escort / handoff**  
   - Direct to Play Attendant or Multi-use / Party room as appropriate.

---

### 4.3 Guest Day Pass – New Visitor

1. **Greeting & qualification**  
   - “Is this your first time here? I can walk you through how it works.”

2. **Brief pitch** (30–45 seconds max)  
   - What Co.work.play is (café + cowork + supervised play).  
   - That we are *not* a daycare; adults remain on-site.  
   - Today’s options:
     - Guest day pass (with price),
     - Membership intro (mention that it’s usually better value if they like it).

3. **Collect information**  
   - Adult name, phone, email.  
   - Child’s name(s) and age(s).  
   - Any allergies / health notes.  
   - Emergency contact (if not the same adult).

4. **Sell day pass**  
   - Process payment.  
   - Provide receipt (digital or printed).

5. **Explain rules & flow**  
   - Wristbands / labels.  
   - Where they can sit with laptops.  
   - Play rules (no shoes where required, no outside food in play, etc.).  
   - Rough time limit / duration of day pass if applicable.

6. **Handoff to Play Attendant / Café**  
   - Introduce them if possible: “This is [Play Attendant’s name]; they’ll help your kiddo get settled.”

---

## 5. Capacity Management

- When **play area** is at maximum capacity:
  - Start a waitlist with:
    - Adult name, child name, phone, arrival time.
  - Provide realistic wait time estimates.
  - Offer:
    - Café-only seating,
    - Option to reschedule if wait is long.

- When **cowork** or **quiet area** is full:
  - Direct overflow to café seating.
  - Note patterns for Manager to adjust staffing/layout.

---

## 6. Safety & Incident Protocols (Host Perspective)

- **If a child is visibly distressed / lost**:
  - Approach calmly, ask name, and if they know where their adult is.
  - Notify Play Attendant and Manager.
  - Make a quiet announcement if necessary.
- **If there is an injury or health issue**:
  - Alert Play Attendant and Manager immediately.  
  - Call emergency services if directed by Manager or policies.
- Document major incidents in the incident log per policy.

---

## 7. Opening & Closing Checklists (Host)

**Opening (before first guests)**

- Lights, music, and scent check (if applicable).
- Host stand stocked: wristbands, pens, clipboards, wipes.
- POS logged in and tested.
- Walk front entry for cleanliness and safety (no tripping hazards).
- Confirm with Play Attendant and Café that they’re ready.

**Closing**

- Sign out of POS and secure cash as per policy.
- Tidy host stand; restock supplies.
- Remove old signage or outdated promo materials.
- Confirm all children and adults are checked out.
- Lock front door (if host is last at entry) or confirm with Manager.

This SOP works together with the Host training videos and checklists in the training system.
