# Franchise Growth Plan Summary

## Purpose
Outline the expansion strategy from the Phoenix flagship to regional, national, and international markets.

## Phase 1 – Prove & Cluster (Year 1–2)
- Open Phoenix flagship and reach stabilization within 12 months.
- Add 1–2 additional corporate or franchise units in Phoenix metro to validate cluster economics and shared management.
- Build franchisor support infrastructure: training LMS, design kit, vendor contracts, marketing playbooks.

## Phase 2 – Regional Expansion (Year 2–4)
- Target Sun Belt and fast-growing metros with high dual-income households (e.g., Dallas, Austin, Denver, Atlanta).
- Focus on multi-unit franchisees to open 2–3 locations per metro; prioritize sites 4–9k SF inline/box retail.
- Launch brand fund campaigns and employer partnerships for caregiver stipends.

## Phase 3 – National Scale (Year 4–6)
- Enter coastal/major markets via regional developers; adapt prototype for vertical/urban shells.
- Introduce smaller satellite formats near dense office clusters as membership add-ons.
- Expand program offerings: camps, tutoring partnerships, and co-branded retail.

## Phase 4 – International Exploration (Year 5+)
- Assess Canada/UK/Australia for regulatory fit and cultural alignment; pilot with master franchise partners.
- Localize policies, menu, and pricing; maintain core brand principles and safety standards.

## Enablers
- Strong unit economics (22–28% EBITDA) support reinvestment.
- Standardized training and Sora content enable consistent quality.
- Modular design kit reduces build-out time and cost across footprints.

## KPIs & Governance
- Time-to-open (LOI to doors) under 9 months; variance tracked.
- Unit-level breakeven by month 6; membership conversion >18% of day-pass visitors.
- Brand compliance audits quarterly; NPS >55 across system.

## Capital Needs
- Fund franchisor team (ops, training, design, marketing) and working capital for first 5–7 franchise onboardings.
- Consider strategic co-investment in early multi-unit deals to align incentives.
